⚠️ RTX A2000 (12 GB) — Workarounds

The 8B model peaks at 16.6 GB — won't fit in 12 GB as-is. Options:

OptionVRAM savingQuality impactEffort
Lower max_pixels to 256×28×28 ~2–3 GB saved Small 1 line change
Retrain on Qwen2.5-VL-3B instead Fits in ~8 GB Moderate Re-run training (same pipeline)
2-bit GPTQ quantization Fits in ~9 GB Noticeable Extra quant step
CPU offloading (device_map='auto') Unlimited None Already set — but ~5 min/inference
Easiest path for A2000: halve max_pixels + enable CPU offloading. Slow but free to run, and electricity at 70W is ~€0.02/hour.

🎯 Recommendations

🚀 Production batch jobs (1k–100k websites)
Use RTX A5000 community cloud on RunPod. Spin up, process, spin down. At $0.16/hr and 28s/website you can process ~129 websites/hour. 1,000 websites costs about $1.24 and takes ~8 hours. Schedule overnight.
💻 Local dev & testing
Any 24 GB GPU (RTX 3090 used ~€600) runs it perfectly. Daily electricity cost for occasional runs is pennies. Good for iterating on prompts, checking outputs, one-off extractions.
🏠 Low-end home hardware (A2000 12 GB)
Halve max_pixels in the inference script + use device_map='auto' to allow CPU offloading. Expect ~3–5 min/website but electricity is basically free (~€0.006/hour at 70W). Fine for processing a few hundred websites overnight. Or retrain on Qwen2.5-VL-3B (same pipeline, fits in 8 GB, faster).
📈 V2 scaling strategy
If this goes to production at serious scale (>100k websites/day): self-host on a bare-metal server with 2× RTX 3090 (€1,200 used). Two GPUs in parallel = 258 websites/hour at essentially zero variable cost. Payback vs cloud in ~3 months at that volume.
